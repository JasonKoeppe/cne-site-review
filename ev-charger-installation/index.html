What nobody tells EV buyers

The charger is the easy part

Three things decide how your install goes — and we sort all three before quoting, so there are no surprises after the truck arrives:

1 · Panel capacity

A Level 2 charger is one of the biggest loads in your house. Older 100-amp services often need headroom math — sometimes a load-management device, sometimes a panel upgrade. We do the math first, free.

2 · The permit

Many local jurisdictions require a permit and inspection for EV charger circuits — and some, like Summit County, have detailed requirements that add lead time. We handle the paperwork and tell you the real timeline upfront.

3 · The wire run

Distance from panel to parking spot drives cost more than the charger does. Photos of your panel and garage usually let us quote the run without a visit.

Timing, honestly: most installs schedule within 1–2 weeks, plus permit time where your county requires one. If you're waiting on a car delivery, call before it arrives and the charger can be ready the week the car is.

EV charging FAQ

Questions EV owners ask us

Is my panel big enough for an EV charger?

That's exactly what the free capacity check answers. Rough guide: newer 200-amp services usually have room; older 100-amp services depend on what else is electric in the house. Send photos of your panel and door label and we'll do the real math for your house.

Hardwired or plug-in — which should I get?

Both are legitimate. Hardwiring supports the fastest home charging rates on many units and removes a plug connection that can loosen with heat cycles; a 14-50 receptacle offers portability. We install both and will tell you what your charger's manual actually calls for.

Do I really need a permit for this?

In many local jurisdictions, yes — a new 240-volt circuit is permitted, inspected work, and some counties (Summit among them) are detailed about EV circuits specifically. We handle it where it's required. It protects you at insurance time and resale, and it's part of doing this right.

Can you install the charger the dealership sold me?

Yes — Tesla Wall Connector, universal units, ChargePoint, Emporia, Grizzl-E, whatever came with the car or you ordered separately. If you haven't bought one yet, we're happy to tell you what installs cleanly for your setup before you spend the money.

What does an install cost?

It depends mostly on the wire run and whether your panel needs work first — that's why we quote flat and upfront from your photos instead of publishing a teaser number that grows later. The quote is free, exact, and in writing.
