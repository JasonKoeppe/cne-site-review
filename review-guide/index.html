How to Review This Build

This is the Astro rebuild of CNE's site, not a mockup: real components, real schema, and a real image pipeline. It replaces the earlier Vite/HTML review build and keeps the same ground rule. This is a review surface only, and nothing here has touched the live CNE website.

What Changed Under the Hood

The build now runs on Astro instead of plain Vite and hand-written HTML: shared layout and components (Base, Header, Footer, Hero, CtaBand, ReviewsWall), typed data files for services, NAP, and reviews instead of copy-pasted markup, and the astro:assets image pipeline, which generates the correct width, height, and srcset for every photo so nothing can stretch or crop wrong the way a few images did on the live site. Client-side JavaScript is a single file, site.js, about 3KB: the mobile nav toggle, tel/sms click tracking, the lead-form handoff, scroll-reveal animation, and the exclusive-open FAQ accordion. No JavaScript framework ships to the browser.

What's Implemented Since the 7/17 Review

  • A full service directory: every service Matt does now has a home on /services/, not just the four paid-traffic landing pages.
  • /free-estimate/ in the main navigation and the footer, next to the phone number.
  • Green reserved for conversion buttons (Free Estimate, Start My Free Panel Check) everywhere on the site, and every button brightens rather than darkens on hover.
  • Every headline and button in Title Case, checked against a character limit so nothing wraps to a third line.
  • Full name, address, and phone (NAP) in the footer and in LocalBusiness schema on every page, matching the Google Business Profile. (v1 deliberately left the street address off; this build restores it, since NAP consistency with the profile matters more than an anonymized posture.)
  • A real review-wall component wired to src/data/reviews.ts. It renders reviews transcribed from the live Google widget, and shows an honest placeholder linking to the real Google listing until that transcription lands. Nothing invented.
  • Each service landing page gets its own photo. No photo repeats across two pages.
  • The lead form and the "text your photos" instructions read as two different things: the form sits in its own tinted card, the instructions are plain numbered steps with no borders pretending to be form fields.
  • Form fields at a normal, compact height, not the oversized inputs from v1.
  • FAQs open one at a time. Opening one closes the others, natively where the browser supports it and with a small JS fallback everywhere else.
  • Scroll-reveal animation on cards and steps, plus animated circuit-line accents, both disabled under prefers-reduced-motion.
  • On mobile, the logo centers in the header and a sticky call bar takes over for the desktop nav.
  • Every em dash removed, replaced with a period, comma, colon, or the word "to" (numeric ranges keep an en dash, like $3,500–$4,500).
  • A full copy pass toward a StoryBrand structure: the homeowner is the hero, CNE is the guide, and every section leads with what's in it for them.

Claims Discipline: What This Build Does Not Say

  • No 24/7, same-day, or emergency-response promises anywhere on the site.
  • No invented review counts, star ratings, or testimonials. The review wall shows only what the live widget transcription provides.
  • No competitor names and no competitor pricing claims.
  • No license number published yet. Every page says "Licensed & Insured, Ohio" until the number clears verification against the OCILB registry.
  • No "family-run." "Locally owned and operated in Tallmadge" is the approved phrase.
  • No fire statistics and no "recalled," "illegal," or "deemed dangerous" language about older panels. FPE copy is limited to the approved wording: the CPSC closed its investigation without determining the panels safe, no recall was ever issued, and independent testing reported elevated failure-to-trip rates.
  • No discounts, percent-off offers, or "cheapest" claims.
  • Solar appears once, in the services directory, in neutral language. No solar promotion.

Production Path

Two viable paths, same as before. Port this build's markup and content into the existing WordPress/Elementor site on the main domain, which preserves current URLs and SEO equity (see research/website-audit-2026-07-17.md for the redirect plan). Or serve this static Astro build directly as the production site behind the main domain. Either way, the lead form needs a real endpoint before launch: this review build hands off to /thank-you/ client-side with no network call, and production wires the same fields to the live FluentForm endpoint (or an equivalent) so a submission actually reaches Matt's crew.
